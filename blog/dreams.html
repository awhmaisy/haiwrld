dreams fly thick and soft over my head

I write like I am drifting beneath the sea. Words come to me as my head breeches the surface, words leave me as bubbles form at my mouth. I collect words and allow them to form a breath, an inhale.

I exhale onto the page in succinct points. I surface onto the sand with a great bellow of thoughts, sentences, incoherent milestones. I form the timeline as I ache to stitch each piece together. I open my chest to feel the river flow in between the rocks of these bullet points.

When the river flows, it tousles each connector and learns to carve story, by connecting. Water pressures stone so often—when I wake, while I eat, before I sleep—and slowly, story appears. I review, I separate my eyes from my sight, I read, I let go. I have written, yes. But I have crafted.
